The journey

Infinity Soft Systems
Day one

Knew this was where I belonged

Knew this was where I belonged. Chose Infinity Soft Systems because of how Megha and Henu Mam talked about where the company was headed. Started putting in work on the Appifany account before the official joining date — before a single day of salary.

First 6–8 months

The only Flutter developer on the account

No team to lean on yet — just the work. Delivered consistently on key client projects, which is what turned a trial period into a full-time offer.

Team grows to 5–10

Mentored Nitin, Mohit, and Trapti from scratch

They joined the Appifany team with little to no Flutter experience. Set up their environments personally, walked them through the codebase, and stayed on it until they could ship on their own. Nobody assigned this — it just needed doing, so it got done.

Steady delivery

Connectable, Mental Floss, Your Fish, Cosmo, Goals FM, Check In Chick — and more

Ten-plus Flutter apps shipped on time with no client escalations through this stretch. That kind of run doesn't happen by luck — it's the same checklist, the same care, repeated project after project.

When it got harder

Become Secure, Good Sam, Agree Hive, Clear Sky, Take A Plate, Rentsy, JobWish

As the team took on more, some projects hit real friction — requirements that shifted late, workload spread thin, not enough hands. A few escalations happened. Instead of stepping back, this is where the extra hours went: unblocking the team, absorbing the pressure, keeping delivery on track anyway.

Multi-platform, self-taught

Picked up React Native, native iOS, and AWS basics on the job

None of this was in the original job description. Learned Swift and Objective-C to cover iOS work and handled AWS deployment basics — because the client needed it and there wasn't time to wait for someone else to learn it first.

Owned end to end

Ran the entire App Store and Play Store release pipeline

Tracked policy changes, resolved rejections, and stood as the bridge between developers, QA, and client stakeholders on every release — the unglamorous work that decides whether a launch date actually holds.

Now — 3 years in

Team Lead, Senior Software Engineer

Company's grown to 120+ people in the time since joining. Leading delivery on the current React Native engagement for Whitetower Digital, still holding the same standard from year one: show up, take ownership, ship it clean.

This is the version of the story that doesn't usually make it into a status update — the mentoring that had no ticket, the projects that got harder before they got easier, the stack learned on the fly because the deadline didn't move.
